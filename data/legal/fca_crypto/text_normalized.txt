FCA CRYPTOASSET FINANCIAL PROMOTIONS REGIME
==========================================

Section 1: Authorization Requirements (FSMA s.21)
-------------------------------------------------

1.1 A person must not, in the course of business, communicate an invitation
or inducement to engage in investment activity unless:
(a) the person is an authorized person; or
(b) the content of the communication is approved by an authorized person.

1.2 For cryptoasset promotions, the following routes are available:
- FCA authorized firms may promote directly
- Unauthorized firms may have promotions approved by an FCA authorized firm
- Cryptoasset businesses registered under MLR may promote to UK persons
  if registered with the FCA for this purpose

Section 2: Risk Warnings (COBS 4.12A.15)
----------------------------------------

2.1 All direct offer financial promotions for qualifying cryptoassets must
include the following risk warning:

"Don't invest unless you're prepared to lose all the money you invest.
This is a high-risk investment and you should not expect to be protected
if something goes wrong."

2.2 The risk warning must be:
- Prominent and clearly displayed
- In the same font size as the most prominent text in the promotion
- Directly adjacent to any investment claim or call to action

Section 3: Cooling-Off Period (COBS 4.12A.18)
---------------------------------------------

3.1 For first-time retail investors responding to direct offer financial
promotions, firms must:
(a) Provide a 24-hour cooling off period before the transaction completes
(b) Send a reminder notification of the cooling off period
(c) Allow cancellation during the cooling off period without penalty

3.2 A first-time investor is defined as a person who:
(a) Has not previously invested in qualifying cryptoassets; and
(b) Is classified as a retail client

Section 4: Appropriateness Assessments (COBS 4.12A.20)
------------------------------------------------------

4.1 Before a retail client can respond to a direct offer financial promotion
for qualifying cryptoassets, firms must assess whether the investment is
appropriate for the client.

4.2 The appropriateness assessment must consider:
(a) The client's knowledge and experience of cryptoassets
(b) The client's understanding of the risks involved
(c) Whether the client can bear the loss of the entire investment

4.3 If the investment is assessed as not appropriate, the firm must:
(a) Provide a clear warning to the client
(b) Obtain the client's express acknowledgment before proceeding
(c) Maintain records of the assessment and acknowledgment

Section 5: Banned Practices
---------------------------

5.1 The following are prohibited in cryptoasset promotions:
(a) Refer a friend bonuses and similar inducements
(b) Claims of guaranteed returns or limited risk
(c) Misleading comparisons to regulated investments
(d) Time-limited offers creating undue pressure

Section 6: Record Keeping (SUP 13A)
-----------------------------------

6.1 Firms must maintain records of:
(a) All s.21 approvals given or received
(b) Risk warnings displayed and client acknowledgments
(c) Appropriateness assessments conducted
(d) Cooling off notifications sent

6.2 Records must be retained for a minimum of 5 years.
