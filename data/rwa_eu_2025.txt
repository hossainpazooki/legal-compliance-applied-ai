TITLE I - GENERAL PROVISIONS
================================================================================

Article 1 - Subject matter

This Regulation lays down uniform requirements for the tokenization, offering,
and trading of real-world assets in the Union, including:

(a) authorization requirements for entities tokenizing real-world assets;

(b) disclosure and transparency requirements for tokenized asset offerings;

(c) custody and safekeeping requirements for underlying assets;

(d) investor protection measures specific to tokenized real-world assets.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Article 2 - Scope

1. This Regulation applies to the following activities in the Union:

(a) tokenization of real-world assets;

(b) public offering of tokens representing real-world assets;

(c) admission to trading of such tokens on trading platforms;

(d) provision of custody services for underlying real-world assets.

2. This Regulation applies to the following types of tokenized assets:

(a) tokenized debt instruments, including bonds and receivables;

(b) tokenized equity, including shares and fund units;

(c) tokenized real estate and property rights;

(d) other tokenized real-world assets as specified by delegated acts.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Article 3 - Definitions

For the purposes of this Regulation, the following definitions apply:

(1) 'real-world asset' means any asset that exists off-chain and has economic
    value, including but not limited to financial instruments, real property,
    commodities, and receivables;

(2) 'tokenization' means the process of creating a digital token on a
    distributed ledger that represents ownership or claims on a real-world asset;

(3) 'asset originator' means the entity that owns or has rights to the
    real-world asset being tokenized;

(4) 'token issuer' means the entity responsible for creating and issuing
    tokens representing the real-world asset.

================================================================================
TITLE II - AUTHORIZATION
================================================================================

Article 5 - Authorization requirement

1. No person shall tokenize a real-world asset for public offering in the Union
   unless that person:

   (a) is a legal entity established in the Union; and

   (b) has been authorized in accordance with this Regulation by the competent
       authority of its home Member State.

2. Paragraph 1 shall not apply to:

   (a) investment firms authorized under Directive 2014/65/EU (MiFID II);

   (b) credit institutions authorized under Directive 2013/36/EU;

   (c) offerings to qualified investors only as defined in Regulation (EU) 2017/1129.

3. The authorization shall be valid throughout the Union.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Article 6 - Application for authorization

1. An application for authorization shall contain:

   (a) a programme of operations setting out the types of assets to be tokenized;

   (b) a description of the governance arrangements and internal controls;

   (c) evidence of the applicant's technical capability for tokenization;

   (d) proof of professional indemnity insurance or equivalent guarantee.

2. The competent authority shall assess the application within 90 days.

================================================================================
TITLE III - DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

Article 12 - Ongoing disclosure obligations

1. Issuers of tokens representing real-world assets shall publish:

   (a) quarterly reports on the performance of the underlying assets;

   (b) annual audited financial statements;

   (c) immediate notification of any material changes to the underlying assets.

2. The disclosure requirements in paragraph 1 shall not apply to offerings
   where the total value of tokens does not exceed EUR 1,000,000.

3. Quarterly reports shall include:

   (a) current valuation of underlying assets;

   (b) income generated by the assets, if applicable;

   (c) any impairments or write-downs;

   (d) changes to the legal structure or ownership.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Article 13 - Valuation requirements

1. Underlying assets shall be valued by an independent qualified valuer:

   (a) at least annually for all tokenized assets;

   (b) quarterly for tokens admitted to trading on regulated markets.

2. The valuation methodology shall be disclosed in the offering documentation.

3. Significant deviations from prior valuations shall be immediately disclosed.

================================================================================
TITLE IV - CUSTODY REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

Article 18 - Custody of underlying assets

1. Underlying real-world assets shall be held by custodians that are:

   (a) for financial instruments: central securities depositories authorized
       under Regulation (EU) No 909/2014 (CSDR) or credit institutions;

   (b) for real property: licensed trustees or escrow agents;

   (c) for other assets: entities authorized by the competent authority.

2. The custody arrangements shall ensure that:

   (a) the assets are segregated from the custodian's own assets;

   (b) the assets are protected in the event of the custodian's insolvency;

   (c) the token holders' claims to the assets are legally enforceable.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Article 19 - Custody agreements

1. A written custody agreement shall be concluded between the issuer and custodian.

2. The agreement shall specify:

   (a) the assets to be held in custody;

   (b) the segregation arrangements;

   (c) the conditions for release of assets;

   (d) liability provisions and insurance coverage.

================================================================================
TITLE V - INVESTOR PROTECTION
================================================================================

Article 25 - Suitability assessment

1. Before offering tokenized real-world assets to retail investors, the issuer
   or distributor shall assess whether the investment is suitable.

2. The assessment shall consider:

   (a) the investor's knowledge and experience with tokenized assets;

   (b) the investor's financial situation and ability to bear losses;

   (c) the investor's investment objectives and risk tolerance.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Article 26 - Risk warnings

1. All marketing materials for tokenized real-world assets shall include:

   (a) a warning that the investment may result in total loss;

   (b) information about liquidity risks;

   (c) disclosure of conflicts of interest.
